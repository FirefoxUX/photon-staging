Message bar

Message bars communicate specific information to a user. Display message bars at the top of the page or the section they apply to.

Usage

Message bars contain concise and scannable text that is directly related to an action that has been completed or is about to be performed. They may include a single message or multiple messages. They should not be used to Dismiss or Cancel. It's required to have an icon always to precedes the message(s).

Common uses for message bars include: success messages, updates, warnings, and problems or errors.

Types

Generic

Convey general information or actions that arenâ€™t critical. Update users about a change or give them advice. Include lower priority information and should always be dismissible.

Success

Inform users when actions are successfully completed.

Warning

Display exceptions or information the user needs to act on.

Error

Communicate problems that must be addressed before a user can complete a task.

Sizes and grid

Message bars have a fixed min-height. Width may vary depending on the content. A message bar cannot grow more than its parent container.

Icon

Icons may provide additional clarity. Icons should be placed to the left of a title or message. These glyphs (16x16) can be found in the icons library.

Icons always have a 4px padding all around.

Behavior

Dismissable

Unless they contain critical information, message bars should be dismissible by clicking or tapping the ghost button, placed always on the right corner.

Call to action

Add one or two micro-buttons in the message bar when you want users to take an action after reading the message.
